THE SYRACUSE FINANCIAL

STUDENT REACTION TO SYRACUSE UNIVERSITY EXTRA EXPENSES

The true cost of getting an SU education when food and transportation is taken into account.

Published on: May 6, 2024

By Sam Albers

Syracuse University charged just over $60,000 for tuition this year. But that's just one expense parents and students face at the University.

The average cost for housing and food is $18,440 for students living on campus, and $17,170 for students living in close by off campus housing, according to Syracuse University estimates.

The university also accounts for another estimated $3,709 for technology, transportation and other miscellaneous expenses.

In an interview with Syracuse local and SU student, Alexander Arefyev, he says his main expense is food.

“I would say that my main expenditures right now are definitely groceries and Ubers, so food and transportation,” he said. “Regardless of what I'm doing in school I always need to pick up food.”

.

From this data set we can see that the largest expenditure category is shelter, with 32.2% of relative importance.

And while the more niche focus of college students' main expenditures may differ from the nationwide average, food and transportation expenses still make up a decent chunk of the total relative importance. Food expenses approximately took up about 14%, while transportation took up about 6%.

After looking at the CPI data and what the university estimated for student expenditures, Arefyev agreed with the obvious importance of consumers and students buying food. However, he highlighted a few key expenses that stand out and exceeded the university’s data points for the average student.

“I think the average student would spend that for sure, and honestly if not more,” he said. ”I’d say that's probably about the minimum.”

Arefyev said that expense that he felt was represented the most inaccurate by the data was transportation, especially for college students.

“Transportation is definitely one that I think where people underestimate spending,” Arefyev said. “It really adds up. You think a $10 or $12 Uber isn’t that much but then you do that three times throughout the week you end up looking at a pretty big sum”.

Transportation expenses were estimated at $768 for both on campus and off campus students by the university. In reality, this expense is likely much more.
